General conclusion

Comedy appears commercially attractive but symbolically undervalued: higher “comedy levels” are associated with stronger box-office outcomes and lower ratings from both critics and online audiences.

At the award stage itself, comedy is not significantly penalized once films are nominated, as winners and nominees show comparable comedy levels at both the Césars and Cannes.

The main disadvantage occurs upstream, where comedy is significantly underrepresented among nominated films relative to plausible pools of eligible films, indicating institutional gatekeeping rather than biased judging among finalists.

Bonus study

An additional study justifying the final point of our conclusion has been conducted. We employed bootstrapping techniques to compare the comedy levels of films nominated for the César Awards and the Cannes Film Festival against a control group of random films.


This study is not hosted on this website as it requires intermediate data science knowledge. For the full report, please find it here: Boostrapping French Festivals
